Statistical Limits on Data Mining
• An analyst can always discover patterns that are meaningless.
• Bonferroni’s principle:
  • You look for events of a certain type within a certain amount of data.
  • Assume random data of the same size.
  • Calculate the expected number 𝑁 of occurrences in the random data.
  • If 𝑁 is large, then you must expect almost anything to be bogus.




                                   Jaemin Yoo                                29
Example: Birthday Problem
• There are 23 people in the room.
• Surprisingly, two share the same birthday!
• Seems like a surprising pattern … but is it?
• In fact, not really, since the probability is quite high (> 0.5).
• Almost half of all such groups will show the same pattern.

When Machine Learning is Useful
• Not all data mining algorithms use machine learning (ML).
• ML is useful when we are not sure what we are looking for.
   • Examples: recommendation, forecasting, and embedding.
• ML is not necessary when we can describe the goal directly.
   • Examples: locating people’s resumes on the web.

Importance of Words in Documents
• Problem: Categorizing documents by their topic.
  • Document is a sequence of words.
• Assumption: Topics are identified by special words:
  • Baseball: “ball,” “bat,” “pitch,” “run,” and so on.
• Observations:
  • Frequency ≠ significance (e.g., “the” or “and”).
  • Rarity ≠ significance (e.g., “notwithstanding” or “albeit”).

Importance of Words in Documents
• Idea: Concentration of useful words in just a few documents.
• TF-IDF (Term Frequency times Inverse Document Frequency).
  • We are given a term 𝑖 in a document 𝑗.
  • TF_IDF 𝑖, 𝑗 = TF 𝑖, 𝑗 × IDF 𝑖 .
  • TF 𝑖, 𝑗 ∝ The number of occurrences of a term 𝑖 in a document 𝑗.
  • IDF 𝑖 ∝ 1 / The number of documents where a term 𝑖 appears.

Hash Functions
• Hash functions are one of the most important concepts in CS.
• A hash function ℎ takes a key and produces a bucket number.
   • The bucket number is an integer, say from 0 to 𝐵 − 1.
   • Normally, a hash key is an integer, and 𝐵 is a prime number.
• Enables us to build a constant-time data structure for big data.

• An index is a data structure for retrieving records efficiently.
• E.g., a hash table where phone numbers are hashed to buckets.

Hash Functions: Questions
• Q1: What if hash keys are not integers?
   • All data types have values that are composed of bits.
   • For strings, there are many ways to convert them to integers.
   • For complex types, recursively convert values to integers.
• Q2: Can we avoid collisions at all?
   • No, there always can be collisions.
   • There are many techniques to reduce collisions though.

Power Laws
• Many two variables are related by a power law.
  • That is, a linear relationship between the logarithms of the variables.
  • General form: log 𝑦 = 𝑎 log 𝑥 + 𝑏
• Examples:
  • Sales of products.
  • Sizes of websites.
  • Wealth of people.
